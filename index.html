GUSG74

Glasgow Urban Sports

Glasgow Urban Sports propose to create a new multi-functional public park beneath the M74 canopy in Port Eglinton. This space will be an inclusive, aesthetically intriguing public space creatively designed with artists to encourage radical urban sports including skateboarding, BMX and free-running: a synthesis of art, sport and urban realm design.

The Community

In a local context, it would act as a physical and psychological landmark, a catalyst for a central area ripe for revitalisation. The site is close by existing transport networks for bus, subway and train routes and offers the opportunity for the exciting new pedestrian and cycling links that the area currently lacks.

Our Goals

This otherwise redundant site has the potential to become an inclusive destination; an appealing, unique and significant local space. With a strong image, sculptural presence and functional yet non-prescriptive architecture, this proposal could set a European precedent by redefining the preconceived notion of what a public park might be.

The Plan

Where's the site?

The site is owned by Transport Scotland, located below the M74 Extension and bounded by Falfield Street, Stromness Street, Salkeld Street and Mauchline Street in Pollokshields area of Glasgow.

  • Multi-purpose public park/ recreational space serving local areas as well as city-wide, national and international visitors

  • Location is central to three southside constituencies: Gorbals, Laurieston and Scotland Street

  • Destination that will create and establish new pedestrian and cycle routes and provide a rare landmark in Port Eglinton/Tradeston

  • Attract cultural tourists, offering a linking destination on route between city centre/ GOMA and Tramway

  • Provide new public 'green space' within an urban environment

  • An innovative urban place that encourages physical activity and enhances well-being, aimed at young people
